Area of a Circle

Interactive visualization showing why the area of a circle is A = πr2

1. The Visual Proof (The "Why")

If we cut a circle into multiple equal slices and rearrange them, they form a shape resembling a parallelogram. Drag the slider to increase the number of slices. Notice how the rearranged shape looks more and more like a perfect rectangle!

8
Conclusion: As n gets larger, the rearranged shape becomes a rectangle.
The height of this rectangle is the radius (r).
The width is half of the circumference (½ C = πr).
Therefore, Area = Width × Height = (πr) × (r) = πr2.
