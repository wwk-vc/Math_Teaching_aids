Before we use math, try building the formula yourself! Cut the circle into wedges and rearrange them.

💡 Drag the colored shapes to move them. Drag the small white knobs to freely rotate them.

2. How it Works (The Slicing Method)

If we slice the circle into many thin wedges and arrange them like you did above, they start to form a perfect shape. Try increasing the number of slices below to see what happens!

  • As you cut more slices, the curvy edges become flatter, and the slanted sides become straighter.
  • Eventually, it perfectly forms a rectangle!
  • The height of this rectangle is exactly the radius (r).
  • The width of the base is exactly half the circle's circumference (πr).
  • Since Area of a rectangle = base × height, the area is πr × r = πr².
